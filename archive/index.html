a curated collection of wallpapers and digital artwork

made in collaboration with cherrybomb & eleventwentysix

the concept

deconstruction isn't destruction - it's creation in its own right. these wallpapers take apart the familiar visual language of computing, stripping away layers until what remains is both more and less than the original.

when you remove color from windows xp's bliss, you don't destroy the rolling hills - you reveal the topology that made millions of people feel at home. when you add grain to macos ventura's waves, you're not degrading the image - you're adding the texture of time and memory.

each piece here is an act of reconstruction through reduction. the goal is to find what survives when everything comfortable is taken away - and discover that what remains is often more powerful than what was lost.
